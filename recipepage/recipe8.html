Directions

  1. Soak the raw almonds in water overnight or for at least 8 hours.
  2. After soaking, drain and rinse the almonds.
  3. In a blender, combine the soaked almonds, filtered water, vanilla extract, and maple syrup (if using).
  4. Blend on high speed until smooth and creamy, about 1-2 minutes.
  5. Strain the mixture through a nut milk bag or cheesecloth into a large bowl or pitcher, squeezing out as much liquid as possible.
  6. Transfer the almond milk to a sealed container and refrigerate. It will keep for up to 3-4 days.
  7. Shake well before using. Enjoy your homemade almond milk in smoothies, cereal, or as a dairy-free substitute in recipes.
